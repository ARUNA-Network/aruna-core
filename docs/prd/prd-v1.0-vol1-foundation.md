# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ARUNA Network is an open-source public blockchain originating from Indonesia designed to provide fair and accessible participation in decentralized infrastructure through affordable and widely available hardware.

Unlike traditional blockchain ecosystems that increasingly depend on expensive mining equipment, large validator operations, or enterprise-grade infrastructure, ARUNA is designed around accessibility and inclusiveness.

The network prioritizes participation through:
* Android Devices
* ARM-Based Systems
* Raspberry Pi
* Mini PCs
* ARM Servers
* Traditional x86 Computers

ARUNA aims to lower barriers to entry while maintaining security, transparency, decentralization, and long-term sustainability.

# PROJECT VISION

To build the first globally adopted public blockchain originating from Indonesia that enables anyone to participate using hardware they already own.

ARUNA seeks to democratize blockchain participation by reducing dependence on specialized infrastructure and enabling meaningful contribution from ordinary users.

---

# PROJECT MISSION

ARUNA exists to:
1. Expand access to blockchain participation.
2. Support low-cost decentralized infrastructure.
3. Promote community ownership.
4. Enable financial accessibility.
5. Create a sustainable ecosystem built on transparency.
6. Encourage open-source innovation.
7. Establish a globally recognized blockchain ecosystem originating from Indonesia.

# CORE PHILOSOPHY

## Accessibility First
Participation should not require expensive hardware.
Users should be able to participate through:
* Android smartphones
* Raspberry Pi devices
* Mini PCs
* ARM servers
* Traditional computers

---

## Community Ownership
The network belongs to its participants.
No centralized authority should permanently control the ecosystem.
Long-term governance should move toward community-driven decision making.

---

## Transparency
All protocol behavior must be auditable.
All treasury operations must be visible.
All economic rules must be publicly documented.

---

## Sustainability
Short-term growth must never compromise long-term survival.
The network should prioritize durability over speculation.

---

## Open Source
All critical protocol components must remain publicly auditable.
Open collaboration is considered a core requirement of the project.

# PROBLEM STATEMENT

Current blockchain ecosystems increasingly suffer from:

## Expensive Participation
Many networks require:
* Specialized hardware
* Expensive validators
* Enterprise infrastructure
This limits participation.

---

## Centralization Pressure
Large operators increasingly dominate:
* Mining
* Validation
* Governance
Reducing community influence.

---

## Accessibility Barriers
New participants often face:
* Technical complexity
* High costs
* Infrastructure requirements
Preventing adoption.

---

## Geographic Underrepresentation
Many emerging regions contribute significantly to blockchain usage but remain underrepresented in infrastructure ownership.
ARUNA seeks to increase participation opportunities from these regions while remaining globally accessible.
